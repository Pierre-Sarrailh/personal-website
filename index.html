Royal connections

Have you ever wondered what the relation is between queen Elizabeth II and Keiser Willhelm I, well if you ever have this is the project for you! In this project I used SPARQL, a query ... language similar to SQL, to query the attributes of WikiData articles (wikipedia's machine friendly interface) in order to extract the familial connections between different medieval European royal figures. Then I used a directed graph implementation where every edge represents a connection between parent and child to show the connections between royals. To show the exact connection between two royals I used a breadth-first and depth-first algorithm to show the different amounts of times each of these algorithms take to search. Rainman

Isn't it the worst when you are driving around following the instructions on google maps and then find out that a road you need to use it flooded, or the snow hasn't been cleared out, or ... it is covered in black ice? well with Rainman you will never have to worry about it again! Rainman is an android app that croudsources weather data from its users and uses it to map road conditions. I used Java, Firebase and various google API's to build this app. The app works by allowing users to create posts in which they describe road and weather conditions and then creates a pin at their position describing the weather conditions.
